About

Purpose of the document — this design document records every decision made while creating OntoGSN.

Goal of the ontology — enable users to create, maintain, align, evaluate, explain and display assurance cases with the help of semantic‑web technology. Key benefits include:

  • native storage and querying of graph data;
  • human‑readable, visualisable domain representation;
  • seamless linking to data, documents and code in one store;
  • logic‑based quality verification through rule engines;
  • a foundation for advanced methods such as GraphRAG; and
  • an active, mostly open‑source tooling ecosystem.

Methodology — every sentence of the GSN Community Standard v3 is translated into OWL 2 triples (classes & properties), complemented by SWRL rules for constraints.

Technical implementation — the ontology is authored in Stanford Protégé 5.6.3, imports RDFS, XSD, Dublin Core, Schema.org and SKOS, and is reasoned over with SWRL‑capable engines such as Pellet or Drools.
